The FIRE (Financial Independence, Retire Early) movement has gained massive traction over the last decade. It revolves around aggressive saving and investing to reach a point where you no longer need to work for money.

## The Core Math: The 4% Rule

The movement relies heavily on the "4% Rule," which suggests you can safely withdraw 4% of your investment portfolio each year in retirement without running out of money. To calculate your "FIRE Number," multiply your annual expenses by 25.

### Example
If you need $40,000 a year to live comfortably, your FIRE number is $1,000,000.  ($40,000 * 25)

## The Two Pillars

Reaching this number requires focusing on two main pillars:

### 1. Increasing Your Savings Rate
Traditional advice says save 10-15% of your income. The FIRE movement encourages saving 50-70%. This requires dramatically cutting major expenses like housing and transportation.

### 2. Investing in Low-Cost Index Funds
Instead of picking individual stocks, FIRE advocates heavily favor low-cost broad-market index funds (like VTSAX or equivalent ETFs). This harnesses the power of compound interest while minimizing fees.

Financial independence isn't necessarily about sitting on a beach forever; it's about buying back your time to pursue what truly matters to you.
